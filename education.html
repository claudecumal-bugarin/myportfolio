My Grade 10 Journey

My Grade 10 journey began with a simple surprise: our entire section received brand-new school supplies. While others marveled at the notebooks and pens, I found myself drawn to the chatter around me—the “Salamat, Mama Joy” and “Ang ganda ng bag” echoing through Room 15. My talkative side (hindi talaga nawala ang daldal ko) kicked in, and soon, I was bonding with seatmates over shared highlighters and borrowed erasers. Those supplies became a bridge, turning strangers into friends who’d later cry over math problems and gossip about crushes. By the end of Week 1, our class already felt like a family—noisy, chaotic, but undeniably kind.

Academically, the year tested me like never before. I’d stay up past midnight, rewriting notes and drilling formulas, fueled by kape and the fear of disappointing my mom. When finals rolled around, I scored a 91% average—enough to graduate with honors but just shy of “With High Honors” in the last quarter (0.875% na lang😭). That tiny gap stung, but my classmates reminded me: “Uy, ang taas pa rin niyan. Tara, ice cream tayo!” Their support turned my “almost” into a lesson—that success isn’t just about grades, but the people who cheer you on.

The best part of Grade 10? Walking on stage at recognition day, hearing my mom’s voice shout “Anak ko ‘yan!" from the crowd. But beyond the medals, what I’ll treasure most are the moments in between—cramming for tests with my best friend, laughing at our teacher’s corny jokes, and sneaking chichirya during lectures. Our section wasn’t perfect (ang ingay namin lagi huhu), but we turned stress into inside jokes and failures into shared memories. Grade 10 taught me that life’s magic isn’t in the big achievements… it’s in the messy, loud, walangya days you spend with people who make it all worthwhile.
