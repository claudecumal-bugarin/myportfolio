My Grade 10 Journey

My Grade 10 journey started with a small but memorable moment—our section got brand-new school supplies. Everyone was excited about the notebooks, pens, and bags, but what I remember most was the chatter in Room 15—people saying “Salamat, Mama Joy!” and “Ang ganda ng bag!” everywhere.

Of course, I couldn’t help talking too. I started chatting with my seatmates, sharing highlighters, and borrowing erasers. Those small moments helped us get closer. By the end of the first week, strangers became friends—the same people I would later laugh with, cry with over math problems, and gossip with about crushes.

Academically, Grade 10 was one of my most challenging years. I stayed up late rewriting notes, memorizing formulas, and reviewing lessons. Coffee was my best friend, and sometimes I wondered if I was pushing myself too much. But all the hard work paid off—I graduated with Honors and a 91% average, something I’m really proud of.

One thing that surprised me was my 94 average in the 4th quarter. Even though it didn’t change my overall grade, it still felt like a win, especially because our school gives certificates every quarter. It showed me that effort matters, even in small increments.

But honestly, the best part of Grade 10 wasn’t the grades. It was the memories: cramming for exams with my friends, laughing at corny jokes from our teachers, and sneaking chichirya during lectures. Our class wasn’t perfect—we were noisy and chaotic—but that’s what made every day feel alive. Those moments taught me that school isn’t just about achievements; it’s about the people you share the journey with.

By the end of the year, I realized that the messy, loud, and fun days mattered more than any medal or score. Grade 10 wasn’t just about academics—it was about friendship, laughter, and learning how to push through challenges with people who make it all worthwhile.
